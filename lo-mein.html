Lo-mein Recipe

Lo Mein (撈麵 / lāo miàn) literally translates to "stirred noodles" in Cantonese. The dish originated in China, with its history tracing back to ancient Chinese noodle-making traditions that date back over 4,000 years. Unlike its cousin Chow Mein ("fried noodles"), Lo Mein is made by cooking the noodles separately until they're almost done, then adding them to the wok to be tossed with other ingredients and sauce. The focus is more on the "stirring" or "tossing" rather than frying. Originally, Lo Mein was served as a soup noodle dish in China. However, as Chinese cuisine spread across the world, particularly in America through Chinese immigrants in the 19th and 20th centuries, it evolved into the stir-fried dish we know today. The American version typically includes more sauce and vegetables than its traditional Chinese counterpart. In traditional Chinese dining, noodle dishes like Lo Mein were considered casual fare, often eaten for breakfast or lunch rather than at formal dinners. Today, it's become one of the most popular Chinese-American dishes, adapted to local tastes while maintaining its essential character of soft, chewy noodles in a savory sauce. The type of noodle used is also significant - authentic Lo Mein uses wheat flour-based eggs noodles, which give the dish its characteristic texture and ability to hold the sauce well.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup oyster sauce
  • 1/4 cup dark soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 cup chopped scallions
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup sliced green onions

Steps

  1. Heat a wok over high heat.
  2. Add the water, oil, sugar, soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, sesame oil, white pepper, ground ginger, and garlic powder to the wok.
  3. Add the scallions, mushrooms, and green onions to the wok.
  4. Stir the sauce until it thickens.
  5. Add the noodles to the wok and stir.
  6. Cook the noodles until they're done, about 2-3 minutes.
  7. Remove the noodles from the wok and serve.
